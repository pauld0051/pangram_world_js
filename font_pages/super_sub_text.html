How it works

This tool is designed for mixed text, so you can keep most of a phrase normal and only change the characters you want.

That makes it useful for chemistry, physics, and maths, where notation like CO₂, x², vₜ, and m s⁻² matters.

Unicode super and subscript coverage is incomplete, so some letters and symbols do not have exact equivalents. When that happens, the original character is kept.

In other words, Unicode is helpful, but occasionally behaves like a caffeinated goblin.
